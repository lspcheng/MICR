Informed Consent

Dialectal Sociophonetic Perception | IRB #: HUM00173998

Principal Investigator: Lauretta Cheng, Linguistics Department, University of Michigan

Purpose of the study

The purpose of this study is to investigate how people perceive speakers from Michigan and Canada, and how people vary in the way that they perceive these different speakers.

Description

In this experiment, you will listen to sentences and/or words spoken by different speakers. You will be asked judge how similar the pronunciations sound by pressing buttons on the keyboard. At the end of the experiment, you will be asked to complete a questionnaire about your personality traits, as well as your demographic and language background. This study will take approximately 1 hour to complete.

Compensation

Upon completion of the study, you will receive $10 in compensation through Prolific.co. At the end of the study, follow the link provided to return to Prolific.co and confirm your completion.

Benefits & Risks

Although you may not directly benefit from being in this study, others may benefit because this research will help us to better understand how people perceive speech. There are no expected risks associated with participation in this study and the topic is not sensitive. We mitigate the risk of confidentiality breaches with regards to your personal data by keeping all data in physically locked and/or password-protected locations.
Your participation is completely voluntary, and you are free to withdraw at any time. If you decide to withdraw early, the information and data you provided will be deleted.

Confidentiality

Your responses will be kept confidential. No one aside from the researchers will see your individual responses. No identifiable information will be attached to any data. These records will be retained for up to ten years.
Your responses will be used for the purposes of scholarly research only. The data may be made available to other researchers for other studies following the completion of this research study, in accordance with data sharing guidelines in the research community but the data will not contain any information that could identify you.
